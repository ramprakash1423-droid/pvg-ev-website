Introduction

A New Way to Charge Electric Vehicles

PVG-EV, a clean-mobility initiative from Prime Ventures Global, is introducing a flexible new approach to EV charging in Tamil Nadu.

Through our strategic collaboration with Setrans, PVG-EV is bringing vehicle-mounted Mobile Charging Stations closer to customers, fleets, businesses and mobility operators across South India.

Instead of depending entirely on fixed charging stations, our mobile charging model brings energy directly to the vehicle, helping reduce inconvenience, operational downtime and charging-access limitations.

Problems Solved

Solving Real EV-Charging Challenges

EV adoption is growing, but access to charging infrastructure remains uneven. Mobile charging can help bridge critical gaps and support both individual and commercial EV users.

Limited fixed-charging availability

Mobile charging can support areas where permanent charging stations are unavailable, congested or not yet commercially viable.

Fleet downtime

Charging vehicles closer to their operating base can help fleet operators reduce unnecessary travel, waiting time and vehicle downtime.

Temporary charging demand

Events, exhibitions, commercial projects and temporary fleet locations may require charging capacity without permanent infrastructure investment.

Emergency charging requirements

Mobile charging can support vehicles that require energy assistance before reaching an established charging point.

Early-stage EV markets

Mobile charging provides a flexible way to test and support demand while fixed infrastructure continues to develop.

How Mobile Charging Works

From request to completed charging session.

01

Request

Customers, fleet operators or businesses submit a charging requirement through the PVG-EV platform, website or service team.

02

Assess

PVG-EV reviews the vehicle type, charging requirement, location, connector compatibility and requested service time.

03

Dispatch

The Mobile Charging Station is scheduled and deployed to the agreed location, subject to operational availability.

04

Charge

The vehicle is connected and charged in accordance with applicable operating and safety procedures.

05

Complete

Charging-session information and service details are recorded for the customer or fleet operator.

Chennai Pilot

Launching the Mobile Charging Pilot in Chennai

PVG-EV is preparing the deployment of a Setrans Mobile Charging Station in Chennai as the first phase of its South India market-development programme.

The pilot will assess customer demand, fleet requirements, charging patterns, operating routes, response times and commercial models.

Insights from the pilot will support the introduction of higher-capacity mobile charging units and wider deployment across Tamil Nadu and other South Indian markets.

Current status: Pilot preparation and partner registration. The network is not live.

Pilot objectives

  • Validate market demand
  • Engage fleet and commercial customers
  • Assess operational routes and response times
  • Test customer booking and service processes
  • Evaluate charging patterns and utilisation
  • Develop scalable commercial models
  • Build the future expansion roadmap

Sustainability

Supporting the Transition to Cleaner Mobility

PVG-EV’s aim is to support the practical adoption of electric mobility by improving access to charging and enabling customers to use electric vehicles more confidently and efficiently.

Mobile charging is not intended to replace permanent charging infrastructure. It is designed to complement it by supporting locations, vehicles and operating conditions where flexible charging capacity is required.
